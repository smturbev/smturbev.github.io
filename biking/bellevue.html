        Bellevue Loop (520 to I-90)

        Notes:
      • Starting Point: UW Rainier Vista
      • Some hills
      • Large portion on neighborhood greenways, light car traffic
      • Distance: ~23 mi roundtrip
      • Estimated Time: ~2 hours

        • The I-90 bridge is a bit nerve-wracking as a person who worries about things falling through the crack between the concrete and the railing. It is also very noisy from the cars speeding by on the freeway. I also always get slightly lost on Mercer Island because the path there is fairly winding with lots of street crossings. Other than that, there are gorgeous view from Bellevue and it was a pleasant ride.

        Brief overview of my favorite biking routes in Seattle

        Most of these bike routes begin near the University of Washington (UW) but if you live anywhere near the Burke-Gilman, you will have easy access to any of the following routes. I've ordered them here by distance/ease.

        1. Golden Gardens

        Notes:

      • Starting Point: UW Rainier Vista
      • Relatively flat and easy out and back
      • Highly trafficked, don't go during rush hour
      • Portion on the road in Ballard because they still haven't connected the Burke through Ballard >:(
      • Distance: ~15 mi roundtrip
      • Estimated Time: ~1.5 hrs

        • You'll definitely want to budget in 30 min or so to hang out at the beach at Golden Gardens because it is gorgeous! This is a great ride for getting started. Low effort, high reward.

        2. Lake Union Loop

        Notes:

      • Starting Point: Gas Works Park
      • One biiiig hill, but pretty short
      • Heavy pedestrian and bike traffic through SLU
      • One short segment on the road because of construction in East Lake
      • Distance: ~7.5 mi
      • Estimated Time: ~45 min

        • You can start anywhere along this loop but you should definitely plan a break at Gas Works Park and SLU Park. Both have great look out points over the water. If you go clockwise, there are slightly less steep up-hills.

        ?. Seward Park

        Notes:

      • Starting Point: UW Rainier Vista
      • Hilly going back north through the arboretum, but not too bad
      • Small portion on the road with light car-traffic
      • Distance: ~18 mi roundtrip
      • Estimated Time: ~1.5 hours

        • Seward park is a gorgeous loop. Closed to bikes during COVID so you can just take a nice walk through the park. There is also a nice little beach area to sit and have a snack.

        ?. Magnolia Loop

        Notes:

      • Starting Point: Fremont bridge
      • Big up hill right before the amazing view in Discovery
      • Small portion on the road with light car-traffic
      • Distance: ~18 mi roundtrip
      • Estimated Time: ~1.5 hours

        • Beautiful vista through Discovery Park and along the waterfront. Definitely need to build up your hill skills before attempting this route.

        ?. Magnolia Loop with waterfront

        Notes:

      • Starting Point: Fremont bridge
      • Big up hill right before the amazing view in Discovery
      • Small portion on the road with light car-traffic
      • Distance: ~X mi roundtrip
      • Estimated Time: ~X hours

        • The added portion is relatively flat and has a great view of the city.

        ?. Bellevue Loop (520 to I-90)

        Notes:

      • Starting Point: UW Rainier Vista
      • Some hills
      • Large portion on neighborhood greenways, light car traffic
      • Distance: ~23 mi roundtrip
      • Estimated Time: ~2 hours

        • The I-90 bridge is a bit nerve-wracking as a person who worries about things falling through the crack between the concrete and the railing. It is also very noisy from the cars speeding by on the freeway. I also always get slightly lost on Mercer Island because the path there is fairly winding with lots of street crossings. Other than that, there are gorgeous view from Bellevue and it was a pleasant ride.

        ?. Interurban North Loop

        Notes:

      • Starting Point: UW Rainier Vista or Gas Works Park
      • Slight long uphill along Phinney Ridge
      • Mainly on trails, some on neighborhood greenways
      • Distance: ~23 mi
      • Estimated Time: ~2 hours

        • There is a nice coffee shop (and public restroom) about halfway through. The long portion along Lake Washington can be a bit monotonous, I can only take so many nice houses on the waterfront and it is pretty flat.
