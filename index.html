TTL Properties

The Tropical Tropopause Layer (TTL), also referred to as the Tropical Tropopause Transition Layer. From 14-18 km in the tropical atmosphere, the tropopause is not a rigid boundary between the troposphere below and the stratosphere above. Instead, this layer has properties of both the troposphere and stratosphere. It has been called the "gateway to the stratosphere."

Cirrus

Cirrus clouds are the wispy clouds you can see on a nice day (aka no cumulus clouds blocking your view). They are made of ice crystals from homogeneous or heterogeneous freezing. Cirrus in the lower TTL are generally thought of as detrained from convection while cirrus lofted in the upper TTL are suggested as in-situ origin cirrus. There is still much debate ongoing around the origin of TTL cirus.
